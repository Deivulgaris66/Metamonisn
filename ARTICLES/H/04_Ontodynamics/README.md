# Ontodynamics — Wayward Metamonism

This section contains the human-readable ontodynamic core of Wayward Metamonism.

Ontodynamics formalizes how physical reality emerges, stabilizes, and evolves from a single ontological prohibition: the impossibility of absolute identity (Axiom 0).

The corpus is structured as a conceptual trilogy:

---

## Ontodynamic Trilogy

### I. Orthogonal Dissipation — Emergence of Nodes

**Orthogonal Dissipation: Logical Unfolding of Reality and Physical Modes of a Single Process**  
A Wayward Metamonism Ontodynamic Article  

Author: Andrii Myshko  
Zenodo DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18678280  

Establishes dissipation as the primary ontodynamic mechanism.

Core results:

- emergence of dimensions through orthogonal discharge  
- mass as unfinished dissipation  
- forces as dissipation modes  
- gravity as shadow mode of global dissipation  
- Casimir effect as laboratory manifestation  
- orbits as compensation zones  

→ Folder: `01_Orthogonal_Dissipation`

---

### II. Orthogonal Compensation — Stabilization of Configurations

**The Principle of Orthogonal Compensation in Wayward Metamonism:  
Ontodynamic Origin of Gravity and the Fine-Structure Constant**

Author: Andrii Myshko  
Zenodo DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18735470  

Introduces Orthogonal Compensation as the geometric stabilization mechanism between two dissipative nodes.

Core results:

- orbits as forced tangential resolution of ontodynamic conflict  
- gravity as shadow pressure of global flow  
- fine-structure constant α as compensation coefficient  
- unification of gravitational and atomic structure under a single mechanism  

→ Folder: `02_Orthogonal_Compensation`

---

### III. Inertia — Internal Dynamics of a Node

**Inertia as Resistance to Resynchronization:  
Ontodynamic Interpretation in Wayward Metamonism**

Author: Andrii Myshko  
Zenodo DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18764852

Defines inertia as resistance to resynchronization between external (1–3D) and internal (5–7D) projections of a node.

Core results:

- inertia as structural delay, not force  
- mass as rigidity of synchronization  
- photon masslessness as absence of internal configuration  
- speed of light as maximal ontodynamic resynchronization rate  
- relativistic effects as saturation of synchronization rigidity  

→ Folder: `03_Inertia_Resynchronization`

---

## Conceptual Closure

Together these three works form a closed ontodynamic architecture:

- Orthogonal Dissipation — emergence of nodes  
- Orthogonal Compensation — stabilization of node configurations  
- Resistance to Resynchronization — dynamics of nodes under change  

Wayward Metamonism thus provides a unified processual ontology of:

- matter  
- inertia  
- gravity  
- atomic structure  
- relativistic limits  

derived from a single ontological axiom.
