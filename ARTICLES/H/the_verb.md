3. Logos as Verb
3.1 The Error of Substantivization
Traditional understanding treats Logos as a noun (law, reason, spirit). Metamonism corrects this: Logos is action relative to identity. It is not a subject or object; it is a verb.

3.2 Operations of Logos
Action of Logos relative to identity has two directions:

diff — negation of identity (distinction)

fix — temporary assertion of identity (fixation)

Both operations are necessary:

diff alone → absolute dissolution (prohibited)

fix alone → absolute identity (prohibited)

Axiom 3.1 (Operational Structure of Logos):

Logos distinguishes (diff)

Logos fixes (fix)

Logos distinguishes distinction (reflection)

Logos distinguishes distinction of distinction (meta-reflection)

Theorem 3.2 (Unbounded Recursion):
The recursion of Logos has no limit; stopping would mean absolute fixation — autoidentity.

3.3 Conflict diff/fix
The diff/fix conflict is the epistemological manifestation of the conflict of autoidentity in Monos.

3.4 Connection Between Logos and Monos

Logos: Action in knowledge (diff/fix)

Both are processes, both are verbs, both relative to identity.
